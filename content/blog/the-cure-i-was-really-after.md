Before I was saved I wouldn't have called it an addiction. Porn was just a thing I did, with a little shame attached, the way most teenage boys have a thing they do with a little shame attached. I didn't think about it much.

Then I got saved, and it got a name. It became the sin. And I made a quiet promise I never said out loud to anyone, this is what the salvation is *for*. Get clean. Get free of this. That was the deal I struck with God, privately, and it was the part of the deal that mattered most to me. More than heaven. More than any of the rest of it.

It didn't work the way the deal said it would. I'd be good for a stretch. Then I'd relapse, and afterwards I'd lie in the dark feeling like the exact thing the verse describes — someone whose feet are swift to run to evil. A loser. Then I'd recommit, properly this time, mean it harder than last time. Then a good stretch. Then the same again, for years. The cycle had a rhythm and I knew every beat of it.

And here's what nobody could see. From the outside I was the model. No drinking, no partying, no sex before marriage. I could tick that box, the big visible one, the one that made me a clean church boy. I passed every inspection the church knew how to run. Underneath the ticked box the thing was exactly where it had always been, and I was the only person alive who knew it.

Then I left. Leaving was supposed to be the honest move, no more pretending, no more box. And the strange part is that the thing I'd brought all that faith in to kill didn't seem to notice I'd left the faith. Same compulsion. I'd given up the whole world the faith came with, and the one thing it was supposed to fix was still there, waiting, unbothered by any of it.

The only thing that changed is that I stopped flogging myself over it.

I'm not the clean church boy anymore. There's no box to tick now and no one running the inspection. But the sad little reality is still sitting there, exactly where it was when I was sixteen and certain that being saved would be the end of it. I went and got saved over this one specific thing. The salvation left. It stayed. I keep thinking I should have something to say about that by now, some way to make it mean what it's supposed to mean. I don't.
