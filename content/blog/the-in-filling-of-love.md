Senior Four, first term, St. Mary's Kitende. My second year there, so I finally knew where everything was and who mattered, and I'd decided the way up the boys' social hierachy wing ran through being on eof the cool kids. What are they doing? A bit of weed and the crazy parties in the school holidays that everyone talks about during the entire school term. It looked cool and I wanted to be part of it. It looked like the short route to being someone confident. I was planning to get cool.

There was a small part of me saying don't, but it was quiet and I wasn't really listening to it.

Then I got into some small trouble, perhaps I missed a class to take a nap, something at about that level, and a teacher was standing over me doing the thing teachers do. And a girl in my class said something. Not a close friend. We barely talked. She said, why do you keep getting into trouble, don't you know there are people who love you.

She didn't say she loved me. She didn't know about the plan in my head to become like the cool kids and start doing drugs. She had no idea what she was interrupting. She was just annoyed at me for being a screwup, and the sentence came out sideways and landed somewhere she never really aimed it at.

I don't have a better word for what happened than fear. Fear and awe. People say "infilling of love" and you picture something warm and soft. It wasn't soft. It was more like the floor moving beneath my feet. A lot of my life rearranged itself in the space of one sentence and I couldn't have told you into what, only that the old plan was off and I was pointed somewhere else now.

I didn't tell anyone. For about a week I just lay in the dormitory at night running it back. Don't you know there are people who love you. I hadn't known. That was the part that wouldn't sit down, not that I was loved, but that I'd been walking around not knowing it, planning my little shenanigans, and a near-stranger had said it by accident.
